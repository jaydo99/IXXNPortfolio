Digispy was a project I worked on for the Internet Design course at university. For this project, we required to create a product or publishment which contributed to the internet culture and related to the content we learnt in the course regaring the internet. For this, I opted to focus on the issue of data harvesting after being inspired by the Cambridge Analytica scandal.

The idea behind DIGISPY was to create something that would get people thinking about where their online data goes, and what it is used for. I wanted to push the idea that ultimately our internet activity ends up somewhere, rather than the common misbelief that it simply vanishes after logging off. Therefore, I decided to pitch the idea of creating a browser-extension called DIGISPY, which visually reflects internet activity back at the user as they use the web.


For this project, I was only required to pitch a prototype of the idea. Therefore, I created a website to present the idea and used javascript to make a prototype of the browser extension. In order to make the website, I went through a branding process to figure out the visual direction of the brand and designed a logo for the product.I also used adobe-effects to make a video to pitch how the extension would work.

Through this project, I learnt how to think abstractly and come up with an idea to push a purpose. I also developed skills on branding aswell as improving on technical skills such as coding html and css. This was also the first time using javascript for a project which helped me to improve in my javascript ability.
